# Genesis deep-audit roadmap — 2026-06-14

> Generated by the `genesis-deep-audit` workflow (wcv48gqyx): 8 internal subsystem auditors + 6 vLLM upstream sweepers (last 30 days) → 115 candidates → 77 adversarially verified → **39 confirmed, 38 rejected** (PN404-class negatives). 92 agents, 6.28M tokens, 31 min.

## Executive summary

Genesis is a mature, heavily-engineered 328-module overlay with a sound STRICT-OPT-IN gating model and strong defensive patterns (P67 safety-gate, backend-gated P108, TextPatcher idempotency). The audit surface is dominated not by missing features but by three recurring failure classes: (1) gating-layer divergence — multiple subsystems (dispatcher dep-graph, PN287, the 19 spec_decode env-bypass patches, PN65) each re-implement enablement checks that drift from the canonical should_apply()/is_enabled() path, defeating the auditability that STRICT OPT-IN exists to provide; (2) anchor-collision risk between patches that mutate the same upstream lines (P23/PN368 on marlin_moe.py w13, PN390/PN369 on rejection_sampler) coordinated only by hand-written prose and undeclared in conflicts_with, invisible to every existing CI/orchestrator guard; (3) dead/no-op/mislabeled wiring that violates the no-stubs iron rule (P24 num_warps overlay, PN102 "pool", PN95 anchor#13/virt machinery, PN65 reversed filter order). The single highest-value correctness item is the version-gate being provably dead at boot (decision.py:84-85 early-return shadows the version check), which is live and bidirectional on the active dev259→dev491 promotion — it both over-skips stale-capped perf patches and under-skips the qwen3coder tool-call wraps that #45171 obsoleted. The highest-value optimization levers are both on P67, the +32%-TPS Ampere hot path: the tf32x3→fp16 PV-dot default and the split-M tl.where state machine — but both are correctly blocked behind a missing spec-decode acceptance harness, making "build the harness" itself a prerequisite deliverable. Several PN404-class negatives were confirmed: P24's "+1.2%" is misattributed Marlin-bsm data, PN102 would TypeError-crash if ever enabled (wrong upstream signature), and P108's "A5000 exposed too" claim is refuted by its own backend gate. Quick wins cluster in observability and registry hygiene (PN65 filter order, PN287/spec_decode gate routing, conflicts_with declarations) — low-risk, high-auditability, arch-agnostic. The dominant theme: consolidate the divergent gating paths and add a mechanical anchor-overlap CI check, which collapses an entire class of latent footguns into build failures.
